Residential Community-Specific Features
Time-of-Use Rate Optimization:

Peak rate periods (5-9 AM, 5-11 PM): Discharge battery to avoid high electricity costs
Off-peak periods (12-5 AM): Charge battery from grid at low rates
Peak rate avoidance saves significant money for residents
50% grid import reduction during peak hours

Load Categories by Resident Impact:

Critical Services (100 kW) - NEVER shed:

Lifts/elevators (30 kW)
Water pumps (25 kW)
Emergency lighting (20 kW)
Security systems (15 kW)
Common services (10 kW)


Comfort Loads (sheddable by priority):

EV charging (120 kW) - Lowest priority, highest sheddability
Air conditioning (280 kW) - High power, comfort load
Washing machines (45 kW) - Deferrable appliances
Common lighting (35 kW) - 80% sheddable



EV Charging Management:

First to shed during outages (zero resident disruption)
Can be rescheduled to off-peak hours
Smart charging to minimize peak demand
100% sheddable without affecting essential services

Resident-Friendly Shedding Strategy:

Prioritizes minimizing disruption to daily life
EV charging shed first (can charge later)
AC shed next (temporary comfort reduction)
Washing machines delayed (can run later)
Lighting reduced but not eliminated
Critical services (lifts, water, security) ALWAYS protected

Grid-Connected Optimization:

Solar self-consumption: Maximize PV usage to reduce bills
Peak shaving: 50% battery discharge during expensive hours
Off-peak charging: 40% charge rate during night (low cost)
Community-wide savings on electricity bills

Islanded Mode:

Lower generator start threshold (20% SoC) - residential comfort priority
Shorter minimum on-time (20 min) - residential generator sizing
Higher restoration threshold (35% SoC) - conservative for resident comfort
Moderate restoration margin (50%) - balance between reliability and comfort

Battery Strategy:

Backup duration: 4.5 hours target
Grid-connected: Focus on cost savings (peak shaving, off-peak charging)
Islanded: Focus on essential services and resident comfort
Smaller battery (450 kWh) optimized for residential use
