University-Specific Features
Academic Schedule Awareness:

Academic hours tracking (7 AM - 10 PM)
Peak shaving during class hours
Different battery strategies for academic vs. off-peak periods

Campus Load Priorities:

Critical (NEVER shed): Research labs, data centers, security systems
Academic buildings: Non-essential during off-hours
Dormitory common areas: Lounges, study rooms (sheddable)
Athletic facilities: Gyms, sports fields (highest shed priority)

Grid-Connected Mode:

Aggressive PV self-consumption to reduce campus electricity costs
Peak demand management during academic hours (40% load support from battery)
Off-peak charging to maintain high SoC for backup readiness

Islanded Mode:

Prioritizes critical research infrastructure
Gen2 only starts when non-critical demand > 100 kW
Higher restoration SoC threshold (45%) for campus stability

Load Shedding Strategy:

Up to 85% shedding of non-critical loads in emergencies
Athletic facilities shed first
Research labs and data centers always protected
Time-based restoration to prevent oscillations

The architecture is identical to the hospital EMS:

Same clean interfaces (MicrogridMeasurements → EMSControlOutputs)
Same state machine logic
Same separation of control from physics
Production-ready for digital twin deployment
