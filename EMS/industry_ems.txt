Industrial-Specific Features
Production Shift Awareness:

Day shift (6 AM - 4 PM): Primary production, full operations
Evening shift (4 PM - 10 PM): Secondary production
Night shift (10 PM - 6 AM): Maintenance/reduced production
Different battery strategies per shift

Peak Demand Charge Management:

Peak hours (9 AM - 6 PM): Aggressive battery discharge to reduce grid import
Off-peak hours: Grid charging at low rates
35% reduction in grid import during peak production
Minimizes demand charges (major cost driver for industry)

Controlled Load Curtailment:

15-minute advance notice before curtailing production equipment
Allows process controllers to safely shut down machinery
30-minute minimum restart time (prevents equipment damage)
Up to 68% curtailment capability while protecting critical loads

Load Priority Hierarchy:

Critical Production (220 kW) - NEVER curtail:

CNC machines (80 kW)
Powder coating ovens (60 kW)
Compressed air systems (35 kW)
Process cooling (25 kW)
IT/ERP/MES (12 kW)
Safety systems (8 kW)


Sheddable Loads (by priority):

Canteen/welfare (lowest priority)
Office HVAC
Assembly lines (can restart)
Material handling
Production HVAC (80% sheddable)

Grid-Connected Optimization:

Peak demand reduction: 35% discharge during peak hours
Off-peak charging: Low-cost electricity during night shift
PV self-consumption maximization
Power quality management for sensitive equipment

Islanded Mode:

Higher generator start threshold (30% SoC vs 25% for hospital)
Longer minimum on-time (60 min vs 30 min) for generator efficiency
Conservative restoration (50% SoC threshold, 20% margin)
Production-first strategy with controlled curtailment

Safety Features:

Curtailment notices prevent equipment damage
Critical production always protected
Restart time limits prevent thermal/mechanical stress
Power quality monitoring for precision manufacturing
