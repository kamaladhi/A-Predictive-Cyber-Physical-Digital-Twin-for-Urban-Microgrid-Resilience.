```
## 🎯 Key Features

### **1. Intelligent DR Allocation**

DR targets allocated based on:
- **Microgrid Priority** (lower priority = higher allocation)
- **DR Capability** (residential > industrial > university > hospital)
- **Current Load** (bigger loads can reduce more)

Example Allocation (500 kW city-wide target):

| Microgrid | Priority | Capability | Allocation | % of Total |
|-----------|----------|------------|------------|------------|
| Hospital | CRITICAL | 10% | 25 kW | 5% |
| University | HIGH | 40% | 125 kW | 25% |
| Industrial | MEDIUM | 50% | 175 kW | 35% |
| Residential | LOW | 60% | 175 kW | 35% |

### **2. Incentive Structure**

**Base Incentive**: $0.50/kWh (configurable)
- Paid for energy reduced during event

**Performance Bonus**: +$0.20/kWh (40% bonus)
- Paid for exceeding committed reduction

**Penalties**: -$0.30/kWh (60% penalty)
- Assessed for mandatory events only if failed

**Example Calculation:**
```
Committed: 100 kW for 2 hours = 200 kWh
Actual: 120 kW for 2 hours = 240 kWh

Base payment: 200 kWh × $0.50 = $100
Bonus: 40 kWh excess × $0.20 = $8
Total incentive: $108

💡 Integration with Priority-Aware Resilience
DR complements resilience policies:
During Normal Operation (Grid-Connected):

DR events reduce grid import
Lower-priority microgrids participate more
Economic incentives align with priorities

During Outages:

DR logic disabled (survival mode)
Priority-based load shedding active
Critical infrastructure protected

During Recovery:

DR can assist grid stabilization
Coordinated load restoration
Prevent grid overload
